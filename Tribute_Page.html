A brief summary of Ambedkar's journey

Early life

    ☛ Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, also known as B.R. Ambedkar, was born in Mhow, India, on April 14, 1891.
    ☛ father served in the Indian army. Ambedkar was the last of the 14 children born to his parents.
    ☛ To ‘Mahar’ caste and considered among the untouchables, he suffered from socio-economic discrimination.
    ☛ However, due to special privileges given to the children of parents serving the army, he received good education.
    ☛ In 1897, he along with his family moved to Bombay where he enrolled at ‘Elphinstone High School,’ becoming the first ever untouchable to receive higher education.
    ☛ Completing his matriculation degree in 1907, he enrolled at ‘Elphinstone College’ in 1908, once again creating history by becoming the first untouchable to enter a university.

Contributions

    ☛ Dr. Ambedkar was a social reformer, jurist, economist, and politician who worked tirelessly toward the upliftment of the marginalized sections of society.
    ☛ Bharat Ratna Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar is known as the “Father of Indian Constitution” as he chaired the Drafting Committee of the Constitutional Parliament. Ambedkar dedicated his life to fighting against social discrimination and untouchability.
    ☛ The Constitution came into force on January 26, 1950, which is celebrated nationally as Republic Day.
    ☛ Dr. Ambedkar was an education-hungry role model for activists with MA, Ph.D., M.Sc., D.Sc, barrister-at-law, L.L.D, D.Litt. and many more educational qualifications. In total, he had 36 degrees and diplomas.
    ☛ He campaigned for the upliftment of Dalits through education, access to public resources, and social integration. His efforts led to the inclusion of fundamental rights and provisions for social justice in the Indian Constitution.

Legacy

    ☛ Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's contributions continue to inspire generations of people seeking equality and justice.
    ☛ His work laid the foundation for a more inclusive and equitable society in India. His birth anniversary, April 14, is celebrated as Ambedkar Jayanti, a day to remember and honor his remarkable contributions.
    ☛ Dr. Ambedkar was awarded with the highest civilian award of the Republic India on March 31, 1990
    ☛ Dr.Ambedkar was the first Indian to pursue a doctorate degree in economics abroad And is popularly known as the “Symbol of Knowledge” and “The Sign of Information”.
